# Paradigm Surface

Distilled pattern: lead with **lived practitioner discomfort**, then **name the shift** so the field can argue with a handle.

## When to use

- You have a structural change in how work is done (tools, stack, profession) and need traction without a product launch.
- You want a post that invites correction and extension, not just applause.

## Steps

1. **Anchor in first-person state** — What changed in *your* workflow this week or month? Avoid generic industry trends until the reader accepts your ground truth.
2. **Name one layer** — Give the new abstraction a short label (new word, or a tight phrase). The label is a compression artifact for discussion.
3. **List the stack honestly** — Enumerate the moving parts you are actually juggling (agents, prompts, tools, permissions, IDE, etc.). Dense lists signal credibility; keep them concrete.
4. **Invite the skill gap** — Frame “catching up” as learnable orchestration, not moral failure. The goal is motion in the replies, not self-flagellation theater.

## Anti-patterns

- Opening with definitions nobody asked for.
- Polished manifestos with no personal timestamp (“we all know that…”).
- Claiming inevitability without a single observable threshold event you can point to.

## Output shape

- One hook sentence (pain or paradox).
- One label for the shift.
- One paragraph mapping old mental model → new mental model.
- Optional: bullet inventory of the new programmable surface.
